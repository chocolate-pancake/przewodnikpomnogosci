Scientific publication list

There are thousands of publications on DID, OSDD or non-disordered plurality. Here we list only the most known and according to our subjective judgement the most important or interesting ones. If you want to read something you don't have access to, there are illegal ways to do it, like using sci-hub and Anna's Archive, which we can't officially recommend.

Warning: most of the papers conteptualise plurality as a division of personality in a singular person.

Filters

*some of the articles not belonging to this category migth have been peer-reviewed

  • Trauma-related Structural Dissociation of the Personality, Ellert Nijenhuis, Onno van der Hart, Kathy Steele, 2004. Warning: regards final fusion as the only treatment option.
  • Fact or Factitious? A Psychobiological Study of Authentic and Simulated Dissociative Identity States, A. A. T. Simone Reinders, Antoon T. M. Willemsen, Herry P. J. Vos, Johan A. den Boer, Ellert R. S. Nijenhuis, 2012. Warning: contains a detailed description of views of people that don't think DID is a real disorder.
  • Separating Fact from Fiction: An Empirical Examination of Six Myths About Dissociative Identity Disorder, Bethany L. Brand, PhD, Vedat Sar, MD, Pam Stavropoulos, PhD, Christa Krüger, MD, Marilyn Korzekwa, MD, Alfonso Martínez-Taboas, PhD, Warwick Middleton, MD, 2016. Warning: contains detailed descriptions of harmful myths upheld by some proffesionals.
  • A systematic review of the neuroanatomy of dissociative identity disorder, David Blihar, Elliott Delgado, Marina Buryak, Michael Gonzalez, Randall Waechter, 2020. Warning: contains a description of views of people that don't think DID is a real disorder.
  • Dissociative part-dependent biopsychosocial reactions to backward masked angry and neutral faces: An fMRI study of dissociative identity disorder, Yolanda R. Schlumpf, Ellert R.S. Nijenhuis, Sima Chalavi, Ekaterina V. Weder, Eva Zimmermann, Roger Luechinger, Roberto La Marca, A.A.T. Simone Reinders, Lutz Jäncke, 2013.
  • The elusive search for a biomarker of dissociative amnesia: an overstated response to understated findings?, Antje A.T.S. Reinders, Lora I. Dimitrova, Yolanda R. Schlumpf, Eline M. Vissia, Sophie L. Dean, Lutz Jäncke, Sima Chalavi, Dick J. Veltman, Ellert R.S. Nijenhuis, 2022.
  • Dissociation in Trauma: A New Definition and Comparison with Previous Formulations, Ellert R. S. Nijenhuis, PhD, Onno van der Hart, PhD, 2011 – for us really valuable due to the fact that two authors of the theory of structural dissociation (often used as an argument against the existence of endogenic systems) acknowledge there the possibility of existence of non-pathological plurality (check the last section).
  • Critiquing the Requirement of Oneness over Multiplicity: An Examination of Dissociative Identity (Disorder) in Five Clinical Texts, Kymbra Clayton, 2005. Warning: discusses forcing fusion and abusive therapy.
  • The Role of Dynamic Core and Mirror Neuron System in Dissociative Disorder, Kenichiro Okano, 2022. Warning: discusses physical abuse, cites sources that are against treating a system as separate people.
  • Dissociative Identity Disorder Adult Treatment Guidelines Third Revision, International Society for the Study of Trauma and Dissociation, 2011. Warning: calls investment in the internal functioning of one's system "narcissistic", advises for treating a system as one person, regards final fusion as the better treatment outcome.
  • Therapeutic Alliance with Abuser Alters in Dissociative Identity Disorder: The Paradox of Attachment to the Abuser, Ruth A. Blizard, Ph.D. 1995. Warning: discusses abuse, including sexual abuse, and self-harm, regards final fusion as the only treatment option, suggests that it's impossible to help certain people, mentions murder.
  • Clinical Presentations of Multiple Personality Disorder, Richard P. Kluft, M.D., 1991. Available only as photos of original document, with text unavailable to screen readers. Warning: discusses sexual abuse, in-system violence, alcohol addiction and being left by a loved one, regards final fusion as the only treatment option, cites text calling investment in the internal functioning of one's system "narcissistic", emphasises that "personalities are not people".
  • The Switch Process in Multiple Personality Disorder and Other State-Change Disorders , Frank W. Putnam, M.D., 1988.
  • Psychophysiologic Aspects of Multiple Personality Disorder, Philip M. Coons, 1988. Warning: discusses physical abuse and self-harm.
  • The ambiguous loss of post-integration : a theoretical analysis of the effects of integration on clients with dissociative identity disorder, Elise A. Manning, 2013. Warning: discusses marginalisation, mentions people disbelieving in realness of DID.
  • Determining medical decision-making capacity for patients with dissociative identity disorder: a patient-centered approach, Timothy Brennan, 2020.
  • Stigma Regarding Dissociative Disorders, David H. Gleaves, Bennett A. A. Reisinger, 2023.
  • Dissociative Identity Disorder: Etiology, Media, and Stigma, Leah N. Millard, 2020. Warning: unchecked.
  • Analysis of Dissociative Identity Disorder Presented in Popular Movies and the Possible Impacts on Public Stereotypes, Suqi Chen, 2021. Warning: dedicated to the topic of stigma, mentions kidnaping and murders.
  • Measuring fragmentation in dissociative identity disorder: the integration measure and relationship to switching and time in therapy, M. Rose Barlow, James A. Chu, 2014. Warning: regards final fusion as the only treatment option.
  • The dream character as prototype for the multiple personality alter, Deirdre Barrett, 1995. Warning: discusses nigthmares and suicide attempts.
  • Dreams in Multiple Personality, Deirdre Barrett, 1996. Available only as photos of original document, with text unavailable to screen readers. Warning: discusses abuse, including sexual abuse and animal abuse, suicidal thougths and various kinds of nightmares.
  • The Phenomenology and Treatment of Extremely Complex Multiple Personality Disorder, Richard P. Kluft, M.D., 1988. Warning: mentions abuse, including sexual and ritual abuse (without major details), regards final fusion as the only treatment option, describes a case of treating a system that seems to not want the fusion.
  • Maladaptive Daydreaming among patients with Dissociative Identity Disorder:A prevalence study, Nirit Soffer-Dudek, Rémy Aquarone, Eli Somer, 2025.
  • Introducing Plurals, Elizabeth Schechter, 2024. Warning: considers arguments both for and against the possibility of existence of many people in one body and other experiences, including the possibility for anybody to have any self at all.
  • Conceptualizing multiplicity spectrum experiences: A systematic review and thematic synthesis, Zarah Eve, Kim Heyes, Sarah Parry, 2023. Warning: discusses stigmatisation and medicalisation of plural experiences and dismissing plural perspectives.
